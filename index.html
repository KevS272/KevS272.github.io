<AboutMe>

Hello there! I am Kevin from Germany and I am currently pursuing a Master's degree in Autonomous Systems with the specialization in Computer Science for Autonomous Driving. The program is carried out by EIT Digital and my two partner university of choice are KTH Royal Institue of Technology in Stockholm, Sweden and my current location, Eötvös Loránd University in Budapest, Hungary.


My professional journey started in the IT service sector with a focus on the manufacturing industry, or, to be more specific, topics like sustainability, Industry4.0 and IT services for the development of automated driving functions. This sparked my interest and inspired my to dive deeper into software development for driverless cars. The experience I gained through my involvement in two Formula Student Driverless teams and countless exchanges with industry experts motivated my to pursue my Master's degree in this exact field. I always look forward to deepen my knowledge in driverless vehicle tech, especially environment perception and deep learning aspects.


I love working on mobile robotics systems, graphical simulations, compute hardware, environment perception sensors and algorithms. Because of my sales background, I also enjoy pitching and presenting my work, motives and goals to whoever is interested.


When I am not working I enjoy going to concerts, making music myself by playing the electric guitar and trying to teach myself to play the piano, exploring or honing my cooking skills, especially my ramen noodles.

Pointcloud projection & cone classification

February 2023

Development of a combined lidar/camera cone detection pipeline for KTH Formula Student's autonomous vehicle. Cone detections from the lidar pointcloud are projected onto the image. Afterward, the area around the 2D point is classified using a lightweight CNN. The implementation was tested using NVIDIA Isaac Sim.
